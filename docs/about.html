About Me

Hello once again, and welcome to my extended introduction. Let's get to know each other a bit more, shall we? (Well, I won't get to know you, only vice versa. That's fine too.)

Where I'm From

I spent my entire childhood in the lovely Green Mountain State, Vermont. For most of my childhood I lived in Bennington, a small town of about 15,000 people that somehow is still the 6th largest city/town in Vermont. This is due to the uh, lack of people in our tiny little state. However, it's got a lot of charm.

Although Vermont is and will always be in my blood, I actually don't live there anymore - I currently live in Concord, New Hampshire (that is, when I am not at school). Also a nice place, although to be perfectly frank I haven't had much of a chance to explore it since I'm only there on breaks from school.

School (and beyond School)

I've been in school for, well, a while now. Right now I am a second-year student at the University of Pittsburgh, where I study Computer Science and Data Science. Right now, I'm just pursuing a Bachelor of Science; I haven't fully decided on grad school or not, but I suspect if I were to go to grad school it would be later on, since I don't have any specific goals that I know I need a Master's or PhD for (yet).

In terms of other fun things I do at school, as you might know from the homepage, I am the secretary for the Pitt Men's Glee Club (I sing lower Baritone). Singing has been a part of my life for at least since high school, and over the years I've bounced between most of the voice parts, from a pre-puberty Soprano voice to a temporary Tenor, and then to a Bass. I've now bounced back up to Baritone - we'll see if I stay there. I only originally got into singing through theatre (musicals being the "gateway drug"), which has been a part of my life for far longer, ever since my breakout role of Peaseblossom in A Midsummer Night's Dream when I was 5. I haven't had as much time to commit to theatre in my college days, unfortunately!

I've also been playing Dungeons and Dragons for a long time (since I was 7 or 8, with my family). I started with 3.5e, mostly skipped over 4e, and now have firmly settled into 5e. This is something that has continued into college - I found a great group through the Rollplayers club at Pitt and we've been gaming since my freshman year, even through Covid. Relatedly, I'm a big fan of board games, specifically the complicated ones. Favorites include Spirit Island, Istanbul, Castles of Mad King Ludvig, Sushi Go, and many more!

Why Computer (and Data) Science?

There are a few reasons I study CS. One very obvious one is that I enjoy coding. Related to that is the sense of puzzling I get from trying to solve programming and CS problems, and the sense of satisfaction I get when that puzzle gets solved. Additionally, CS is a very broad field, which appeals to me because I'm still not sure what I'd like to do with my life and my career; I know I have some flexiblity with my degree.

As for Data Science, that sort of just worked out. The major is very new to Pitt and was introduced between my first and second years, and I noticed that the overlap between it and CS was pretty heavy and I had already taken or planned to take many of the courses due to personal interest, so it seemed like a good fit and a way to refine my interests.
